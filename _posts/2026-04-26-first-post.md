# Welcome

Hello and welcome to all who enter this site! My name is Nader Hdeib and I am a recent graduate of the Bachelor of Electrical/Computer Engineering Program at Dalhousie University. I made this site to begin documenting projects that I work on in my free time.

As you can probably tell from the paragraph above, I am mainly interested in embedded systems engineering and have fun tinkering on both the firmware/software side and the hardware side of things. I am familiar with multiple RTOS's, mainly FreeRTOS and Zephyr, and have experience developing embedded software on multiple platforms including the STM32G series microcontrollers (bare metal), NRF54 SoC's (Zephyr), and SAMG55, D20, and V71 microcontrollers (FreeRTOS or bare-metal). I have also dabbled slightly in FPGA design through university courses, but still have a long way to go before calling it a skill.

I plan on expanding my skillset as time goes on to include more hardware design (I have dabbled with some 2-4 layer boards in KiCad), and to improve my software/firmware development practices. If you are interested in all things embedded, including toolchain setup, hardware design, firmware design, and anything else "embedded" entails, follow along on this page to see what projects I have been up to!

# What's to Come
I don't want to promise too much (or spoil anything cool), but I do have plans to complete a few RTOS projects for personal learning, and possibly as open source tools for anyone to use. Stay tuned to find out what I make!
